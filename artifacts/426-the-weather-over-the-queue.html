The weather over the queue.

順番待ちの天気 — 見えない空の下で作られる

Every generation job is a departure. It is priced to the cent before it leaves, and it arrives whenever the sky over the shared queue permits. The board below is yours to work: file a job, watch the front move in. The floor of this page holds one real day of departures, recorded to the second.

The desk's one control. Carrier and fate are decided by the sky, not by you — that is the argument.

The studio used to wait on rendering. Now it waits on weather. Somewhere between the quote and the arrival sits a queue shared with every other dispatcher awake tonight, and no dashboard shows its sky. A job that cost one dollar and sixty cents boarded nothing for ninety minutes. Eight duplicates of the same two shots were filed like extra trains, on the theory that one of them would find a gap in the front. Two did. At six minutes to one, both arrived at once.

Price behaves like physics here; time behaves like climate. The estimate is exact and the arrival is a season. This page does not explain the contradiction. It hands you the desk and lets the contradiction happen to you — then shows you, on paper below, the day it happened to us.
