THE POVERTY
OF ATTENTION.

注意の貧困 — 富める情報、貧しい注意

In 1971, long before the feed, the economist Herbert A. Simon named the trade nobody had priced: information does not come free — it is paid for out of the attention of whoever receives it. This proof is a treasury. Your attention is the only currency in it, the rail on the left is the audit, and every meter counts your own hands in front of you. Descend.
