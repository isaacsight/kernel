Footnotes System

Micro-information design compresses meaning1 into the smallest possible footprint while maintaining clarity. It rewards attention2 rather than demanding it.

  1. Compression without loss of fidelity is the mark of expertise.
  2. The difference between "demanding" and "rewarding" attention is the difference between advertising and craft.
Core Principles
Clarity
"Everything visible, nothing loud."
Density
"Information earns its space."
Restraint
"One accent per view."
Density Modes
Compact
Dense information display for power users who want maximum data per viewport.
Default
Balanced density for everyday use. Comfortable scanning with good readability.
Reading
Expanded for long-form content. Generous line height and spacing.
