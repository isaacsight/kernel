# Pattern: The Alignment Lens

**We don't just ship code. We ship decisions. The Alignment Lens is a quick diagnostic to check if those decisions serve the human or the machine.**

## The Principle
In an AI studio, speed is easy. *Direction* is hard.
It is tempting to let the agents run wild, generating thousands of pages or features.
The Alignment Lens forces you to pause and ask: **"Does this feel right?"**

## The Diagnostic (5 Questions)

Before launching an AI feature or agent, ask these 5 questions. If the answer is "No" to any of them, **stop**.

1.  **Does it respect the user's attention?**
    *   *Yes:* It summarizes complexity and saves time.
    *   *No:* It floods them with generated noise just because it can.

2.  **Is the "Human in the Loop" meaningful?**
    *   *Yes:* The human makes the final high-stakes decision.
    *   *No:* The human is just a rubber stamp for the machine's work.

3.  **If the AI fails, does it fail gracefully?**
    *   *Yes:* It admits it doesn't know or falls back to a safe default.
    *   *No:* It hallucinates a confident lie that breaks trust.

4.  **Do we own the outcome?**
    *   *Yes:* We understand *why* the system made the decision.
    *   *No:* It's a "black box" and we can't explain it to a customer.

5.  **Does it make the team smarter?**
    *   *Yes:* It handles rote work so we can think deeply.
    *   *No:* It makes us lazy and we are forgetting how our own product works.

## When to Use It
*   **Weekly Review**: Look at the last week's shipping through this lens.
*   **Spec Review**: Before writing code, apply the lens to the feature request.

## The Promise
If you build with this lens, you won't just build *faster*. You will build things that people actually want to live with.
