# Agentic Systems Engineering
**Agentic Systems Engineering (ASE)** is the formal discipline of building, orchestrating, and governing the "nervous systems" of AI-native organizations. 

It is not pure AI research—we are not training foundation models. It is not pure organization design—we are not just moving boxes on a chart. It is the engineering of the **orchestration layer** where models, tools, and human judgment meet.

ASE treats the "operating system" of a studio or team as a first-class software concern, requiring telemetry, decision policies, and compounding intelligence.

---

## The Four Pillars

### 1. AI Engineering
ASE involves the technical orchestration of agents, workflows, and guardrails. This includes:
- **Agent Architecture**: Designing Moore-neighbor-style or hierarchical agent teams with discrete roles.
- **Workflow Automation**: Connecting high-level goals to concrete tool executions.
- **Guardrails**: Implementing verifiable constraints to ensure agent actions remain within safe and productive bounds.

### 2. Human-AI Collaboration
ASE designs the **decision-making loops** where humans and agents share responsibility.
- **Decision Policies**: Formalizing when an agent can act autonomously vs. when it must defer to human judgment.
- **Interaction UX**: Building the interfaces (like the Studio extension) that allow humans to capture signals and review agent logic in real-time.
- **Taste & Judgment**: Ensuring the human "felt right" signal is the ultimate regulator of the system.

### 3. Computational Organization Theory
ASE models how AI-native teams structure roles, rituals, and metrics.
- **Dynamic Roles**: Defining how agents take on work traditionally held by human roles (e.g., Librarian, Trend Scout).
- **Rituals as Loops**: Turning recurring meetings or processes into automated update cycles (like the Evolution Loop).
- **Efficiency Metrics**: Moving beyond vanity stats to measure the true leverage of the agentic layer.

### 4. AI Governance
ASE ensures alignment, safety, and verifiability in agentic systems. This is the home of the **[DTFR Evaluation Harness](/dtfr-harness.html)**.
- **Felt-Sense Evaluation**: Moving beyond binary pass/fail to record how an agentic action "feels" against human standards.
- **Immutable Logging**: Documenting every agent decision in verifiable logs.
- **Alignment Monitoring**: Detecting when agent behavior drifts from core studio values using the *Felt Right Index*.

## Why this discipline?

The current wave of AI is entering the "implementation gap." Models are powerful, but organizations are struggling to integrate them without creating chaos or fragile, opaque workflows.

Agentic Systems Engineering provides the framework to transition from "AI as a feature" to "AI as the core operating system"—delivering systems that are **observable, forkable, and verifiable**.
