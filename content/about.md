Does This Feel Right? is a small frontier studio that runs on its own operating system first—and invites other builders to watch, copy, or commission that system, not generic services.[[1]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com)

## What this studio is

This is not an agency or a content brand. It is a tightly scoped studio-of-one that uses AI agents, automations, and opinionated workflows to run real work: thinking, writing, building, and deciding.[[2]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com/posts/studio-os.html)[[3]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com/posts/research-roadmap.html)
The primary product is the Studio OS itself—a live, evolving configuration of tools, agents, rituals, and metrics that makes one human unusually effective in an AI-saturated world.[[3]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com/posts/research-roadmap.html)[[4]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com/posts/the-frontier-team.html)[[2]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com/posts/studio-os.html)

## Where it sits in the AI stack

The giants—OpenAI, Anthropic (Claude), Google (Gemini), Perplexity, and others—provide foundation models, tools, and ecosystems.[[5]](https://xenoss.io/blog/openai-vs-anthropic-vs-google-gemini-enterprise-llm-platform-guide)[[6]](https://productstudio.substack.com/p/openai-vs-anthropic-a-detailed-comparison)[[7]](https://www.promptitude.io/post/navigating-the-ai-landscape-openai-vs-anthropic-vs-google-ai-in-2024)[[8]](https://beam.ai/agentic-insights/the-rise-of-ai-ecosystems-lessons-for-startups-building-on-foundation-models)
Does This Feel Right sits one layer above them as an agentic OS and taste layer: a specific, lived way of orchestrating multiple models and tools into a coherent studio, avoiding lock-in and chaos.[[8]](https://beam.ai/agentic-insights/the-rise-of-ai-ecosystems-lessons-for-startups-building-on-foundation-models)[[9]](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/quantumblack/our-insights/seizing-the-agentic-ai-advantage)[[10]](https://www2.eecs.berkeley.edu/Pubs/TechRpts/2024/EECS-2024-223.pdf)[[11]](https://arxiv.org/html/2503.23037v1)

## Who it is for

The studio is for founders and product/engineering leads who:

- Have already shipped at least one AI-inflected feature, workflow, or internal agent.[[12]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com/posts/ai-meet-your-ai-engineering-team.html)[[13]](https://menlovc.com/perspective/2025-the-state-of-generative-ai-in-the-enterprise/)
- Can feel that their system is getting messy—misalignment between vision, UX, architecture, and business metrics—even if dashboards look “fine.”[[14]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com/posts/studio-os-6-week-journey-week-1.html)[[15]](https://fortune.com/2025/08/18/mit-report-95-percent-generative-ai-pilots-at-companies-failing-cfo/)[[16]](https://deepsense.ai/blog/why-75-of-ai-initiatives-fail-and-how-misaligned-business-tech-collaboration-is-the-root-cause/)[[2]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com/posts/studio-os.html)[[12]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com/posts/ai-meet-your-ai-engineering-team.html)
- Want a sharper operating system for their team, not another slide deck or generic consultant.

These are small-to-mid teams (or serious product groups) that care about taste, leverage, and long-term control more than performative “AI transformation.”[[1]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com)[[8]](https://beam.ai/agentic-insights/the-rise-of-ai-ecosystems-lessons-for-startups-building-on-foundation-models)

## The core problem it exists to tackle

Modern AI products and orgs tend to fail not on raw model quality, but on misalignment and system sprawl:

- AI and automation get bolted on to existing products, creating fragile, opaque workflows that few people truly understand.[[15]](https://fortune.com/2025/08/18/mit-report-95-percent-generative-ai-pilots-at-companies-failing-cfo/)[[16]](https://deepsense.ai/blog/why-75-of-ai-initiatives-fail-and-how-misaligned-business-tech-collaboration-is-the-root-cause/)[[2]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com/posts/studio-os.html)[[12]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com/posts/ai-meet-your-ai-engineering-team.html)[[14]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com/posts/studio-os-6-week-journey-week-1.html)
- Teams optimize local metrics (latency, usage, tickets closed) while eroding the underlying experience, trust, and economics.[[17]](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/ai-misalignment-where-enterprise-workflows-product-suman-chittimuri-lxtdc)[[18]](https://www.mindtheproduct.com/why-most-ai-products-fail-key-findings-from-mits-2025-ai-report/)[[19]](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/ai-isnt-riskmisalignment-strategic-guide-boards-shane-culbertson-ttgpe)[[15]](https://fortune.com/2025/08/18/mit-report-95-percent-generative-ai-pilots-at-companies-failing-cfo/)

Does This Feel Right exists to demonstrate and export a different pattern: small, intelligible, high-leverage systems that “feel right” to their users, builders, and numbers.

## How the studio operates internally

The studio treats itself as its first and most important client:

- It runs a Studio OS composed of:  
  - A Frontier Team (human + AI agents with named roles).[[4]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com/posts/the-frontier-team.html)[[20]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com/posts/hiring-the-frontier-team.html)
  - Workflows for research, thinking, drafting, publishing, and decision-making.[[21]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com/tags/research.html)[[3]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com/posts/research-roadmap.html)
  - A minimal, non-vanity metric set (shipping cadence, rework, reuse, automation share).[[22]](https://axify.io/blog/engineering-metrics)[[23]](https://jellyfish.co/blog/engineering-kpis/)

- It instruments these systems enough to know what is actually working: which patterns save time, improve quality, or reduce chaos—and which should be deprecated.[[16]](https://deepsense.ai/blog/why-75-of-ai-initiatives-fail-and-how-misaligned-business-tech-collaboration-is-the-root-cause/)[[24]](https://auditboard.com/blog/how-ai-maturity-impacts-risk-speed-and-strategy)[[15]](https://fortune.com/2025/08/18/mit-report-95-percent-generative-ai-pilots-at-companies-failing-cfo/)
- It maintains a public studio snapshot and changelog so others can see how the OS evolves over time.[[2]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com/posts/studio-os.html)[[3]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com/posts/research-roadmap.html)[[4]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com/posts/the-frontier-team.html)

The internal rule: ideas that don’t survive real use inside the studio do not become patterns for anyone else.

## What gets exposed publicly

The site exposes only the sharpest abstractions from this internal OS:

- Studio Snapshot  
  A live view of the studio’s focus, active systems/agents, and a handful of metrics that matter.[[3]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com/posts/research-roadmap.html)[[2]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com/posts/studio-os.html)

- Patterns  
  Named, opinionated building blocks (e.g., Frontier Team v1, Research OS, Studio OS spine) that describe:  
  - The problem they solve and for whom.  
  - When to use them—and when not.  
  - Diagrams and minimal implementation details (stack assumptions, events, cadences).[[20]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com/posts/hiring-the-frontier-team.html)[[4]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com/posts/the-frontier-team.html)[[2]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com/posts/studio-os.html)[[3]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com/posts/research-roadmap.html)

- Retired moves  
  Patterns and experiments that were tried, measured, and intentionally killed, with short notes on why.[[3]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com/posts/research-roadmap.html)

Essays and logs remain, but serve this OS: they narrate the evolution of the studio, its systems, and its bets.[[25]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com/experiments.html)[[3]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com/posts/research-roadmap.html)

## How others can engage

Everything routes through three verbs:

- Watch  
  Follow the studio snapshot, changelog, and essays to see how one frontier studio actually runs day to day.[[1]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com)[[3]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com/posts/research-roadmap.html)

- Copy  
  Take specific patterns and utilities (diagrams, workflows, configs) and adapt them to your own stack and team.[[2]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com/posts/studio-os.html)[[3]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com/posts/research-roadmap.html)

- Commission  
  For a small number of aligned teams, commission a custom frontier system: a Studio OS spine or Frontier Team design tailored to their product and stack, using the studio’s own OS as the reference implementation.[[4]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com/posts/the-frontier-team.html)[[3]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com/posts/research-roadmap.html)

Commissions are not ongoing “fractional” roles; they are focused system-design engagements: one or a few flows, one OS spine, one clear before/after.

## What the studio refuses to do

To stay intelligent and sharp, the studio deliberately avoids:

- Generic “AI strategy” decks unmoored from running systems.[[18]](https://www.mindtheproduct.com/why-most-ai-products-fail-key-findings-from-mits-2025-ai-report/)[[15]](https://fortune.com/2025/08/18/mit-report-95-percent-generative-ai-pilots-at-companies-failing-cfo/)[[16]](https://deepsense.ai/blog/why-75-of-ai-initiatives-fail-and-how-misaligned-business-tech-collaboration-is-the-root-cause/)
- Long-term, indefinite implementation contracts that turn it into staff augmentation.  
- Engagements where leadership is unwilling to change structure or workflows in response to what the OS reveals.

The thesis is simple: by running the cleanest, most opinionated frontier studio it can for itself—and making that OS partially legible—Does This Feel Right can give other builders something rarer than another tool or talk: a concrete, living example of an AI-native way of working that actually feels right.[[4]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com/posts/the-frontier-team.html)[[1]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com)[[2]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com/posts/studio-os.html)[[3]](https://www.doesthisfeelright.com/posts/research-roadmap.html)
