Today was a productive day for the **Studio OS**. We focused heavily on refining the core user experience and ensuring the content architecture can scale as we ramp up production.

## Key Improvements

### 1. Pagination & Scalability
We successfully implemented pagination for both the **Latest Essays** feed and the **Experiments** archive. This was a critical step to ensure the site remains performant and navigable as we add more content. No more infinite scroll fatigue—just clean, composed pages.

### 2. Homepage Information Architecture
The homepage received a significant structural update:
*   **Start Here**: A distinct section for our "Starter Set" to guide new visitors.
*   **Experiments**: Promoted to a core section, acknowledging that raw ideas and technical notes are as valuable as polished essays.
*   **Visual Hierarchy**: We quieter metadata styles and cleaner tag presentations to let the content titles breathe.

### 3. Visual Polish
We dived deep into `style.css` to refine the typography and spacing. The goal was to reduce "feed fatigue" and align with a more premium, "Studio" aesthetic.

### 4. Backend & Tooling
Behind the scenes, we fixed several issues in our build pipeline, including tag parsing and date rendering. We also laid the groundwork for the **Hybrid Video Engine** and continued our research into **Collective AI** architectures.

## What's Next?
We're now generating a massive dataset of experiments and workshops to stress-test our new pagination and layout logic. The goal is to see how the system handles effectively 100+ nodes of content.

Stay tuned.
