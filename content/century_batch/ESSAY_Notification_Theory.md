# The War for Attention

Your phone is a slot machine. Every notification is a pull of the lever.
For an AI OS, this is a problem. If the AI notifies you every time it has a thought, you will mute it.
We needed a **Notification Doctrine**.

## The Three Tiers of Urgency
1.  **Info (Silent)**: "I finished indexing the documentation." (Show in badge only).
2.  **Action Required (Buzz)**: "I need your approval to deploy." (Vibrate).
3.  **Critical (Alarm)**: "The production server is down." (Sound).

## Contextual Gating
The system knows *where* you are (Geofencing) and *what* you are doing (Calendar).
*   **Deep Work Mode**: If your calendar says "Focus," the AI holds all Tier 2 notifications.
*   **Sleep Mode**: The Guardian suppresses everything except Tier 3 Criticals.

## The Daily Brief
Instead of 50 small notifications, the **Librarian** compiles a "Daily Brief" delivered at 8 AM.
"While you slept, I fixed 3 bugs, found 2 papers, and drafted 1 post."
One notification. High value. Zero noise.
