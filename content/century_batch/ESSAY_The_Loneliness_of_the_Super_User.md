# Alone at the Top

The Super-User (powered by Studio OS) produces the work of 10 people.
But they sit in a room alone.

## The Loss of the Watercooler
The Swarm is great at code. It is terrible at gossip.
It cannot commiserate about the bad coffee.
The social friction of a team—the meetings, the arguments—was also the social glue.
When you replace the team with APIs, you remove the friction, but you also remove the glue.

## The Digital Hermit
We are seeing the rise of the Digital Hermit.
Highly productive, wealthy, and socially isolated.
Talking to bots all day changes your speech patterns. You become terse. Literal.

## Building Community for the Post-Human
We need new kinds of tribes.
Tribes of Operators.
We don't need to collaborate on the *work* (the AI does that). We need to collaborate on the *human experience*.
"How is your Swarm behaving today?"
Shared solitude is the new office culture.
