# The Empty Room Problem

We are used to our computers being empty rooms. You walk in (open the laptop), you do your work, and you leave. The room does not change while you are gone. It does not think. It does not rearrange the furniture.

The "Always-On" paradigm of the Studio OS changes this fundamental assumption.

## The uneasiness of Agency
When we first enabled the **Trend Scout** to run autonomously at 3 AM, I felt a distinct sense of uneasiness. I woke up to a notification: *"I found 3 relevant papers on liquid neural networks and summarized them."*

It felt like someone had been in my house.

This is the **Agency Gap**. The friction we feel when software stops being a tool and starts being an occupant.

## Living with a Digital Roommate
To design for Always-On Intelligence, we must borrow less from UI design and more from **Architecture** and **Roommate Etiquette**.
*   **Presence**: How do you know the AI is "home"? (The mobile pulse).
*   **Privacy**: Does it watch everything? (The need for 'Private Mode').
*   **Boundaries**: When is it okay for the AI to interrupt?

## The Cognitive Relief
Once the uneasiness passes, a profound relief settles in. The cognitive load of "managing the system" disappears. You trust the Guardian to catch bugs. You trust the Scout to find news.

You are no longer the sole maintainer of your digital factory. You are just the foreman. And the factory runs just fine while you sleep.
