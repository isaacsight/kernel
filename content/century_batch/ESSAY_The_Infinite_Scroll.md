# The Bottomless Page

Pagination is a legacy of books. "Turn the page."
The internet is a scroll.
What if the scroll never ended? Not because of more ads, but because of **Real-time Generation**.

## Just-in-Time Content
As you reach the bottom of an essay, the **Research Agent** asks:
*"Are you interested in the counter-argument?"*
If you keep scrolling, it *generates* the counter-argument on the fly.
The essay has no fixed length. It is as long as your curiosity.

## The Oracle Pattern
This shifts the web from "Archive" (reading what was written) to "Oracle" (generating what is needed).
The site is not a library. It is a conversation partner that types very fast.

## Curation is Key
The danger is sludge. Infinite low-quality text.
This is where the **Editor** agent is critical. It must ruthlessly cut, condense, and stop the scroll if quality drops.
Infinite does not mean indiscriminate.
