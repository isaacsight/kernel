# Stop Waiting for Me

The defining characteristic of current software is politeness. It waits for you to click. It waits for you to type. It never speaks unless spoken to.

In a Swarm System, this politeness is a bug.

## The Case for Interruption
Imagine a colleague who watches you make a mistake, lays the groundwork for a disaster, and says nothing because you didn't ask "Am I making a mistake?"
You would fire them.

Yet we accept this from our IDEs.
The **Interruptible Interface** is a paradigm where the AI has the permission to stop you.
*   **"Hold on, that variable is undefined."**
*   **"Wait, this deployment will break the staging environment."**

## Designing the Tap on the Shoulder
The challenge is not detecting the error; it's designing the interruption.
*   **The Modal is Evil**: A popup box is a slap in the face.
*   **The Peripheral Glow**: A gentle red pulse in the corner of the screen. It signals: "I have an objection, but I won't stop your hands."
*   **The Haptic Nudge**: A vibration on the mobile tether.

## Bi-Directional Flow
Communication is a two-way street. Currently, we have a one-way command line.
We are building a **Conversation**. Sometimes I talk. Sometimes the System talks.
We must normalize the idea that the computer has something to say.
