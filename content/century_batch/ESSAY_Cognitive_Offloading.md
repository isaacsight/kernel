# The Calculator Effect

When calculators appeared, math teachers panicked. "They won't know arithmetic!"
They were right. We got worse at arithmetic.
But we got better at **Mathematics**.

## The Atrophy of Rote Memory
I don't know my friends' phone numbers. My phone knows them.
I don't know the syntax for `kubectl`. My AI knows it.
My rote memory has atrophied.

## The Hypertrophy of Synthesis
But my ability to connect concepts has exploded.
Because I am not wasting RAM on syntax, I can hold the entire system architecture in my head.
I trade "Micro-Competence" for "Macro-Understanding."

## The Net Gain
Are we getting dumber?
If you measure "Dumb" by "Can you solve a specific problem without tools?" -> Yes.
If you measure "Smart" by "What complexity of problem can you solve *with* tools?" -> We are infinitely smarter.
We are optimizing for the cyborg, not the naked ape.
