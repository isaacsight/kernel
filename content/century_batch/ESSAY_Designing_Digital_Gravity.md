# Use vs. Immersion

Most software is designed to be used. You open Excel to make a spreadsheet. You open Uber to get a ride. The relationship is transactional.

The Studio OS is designed for **Gravity**.

## What is Digital Gravity?
Digital Gravity is the quality of a system that pulls you into its orbit. It is the defining characteristic of an OS that wants to be a "Second Brain."
*   **Low Friction**: It takes fewer clicks to engage than to not engage.
*   **High Context**: It already knows what you are working on.
*   **Aesthetic Pleasure**: It feels good to be there.

## The Dark Mode Aesthetic & The Glow
We chose a deep, rich dark mode not just for battery life, but for atmosphere. The Studio is a cockpit.
*   **The Pulse**: Interactive elements shouldn't just toggle; they should glow. They should breathe.
*   **Motion**: Transition states (swapping agents) are not jarring cuts; they are fluid morphs.

## The Dashboard as a Living Space
In a transactional app, empty states are blank. "No messages."
In the Studio OS, the dashboard is never empty. It shows the **Swarm's Heartbeat**.
*"Architect is idle. Researcher is reading. Guardian is watching."*
Even when you are doing nothing, the system is alive. This gravity keeps you connected, making the "Shift to Mobile" feel less like leaving work and more like taking the work with you.
