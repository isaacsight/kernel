# The Tyranny of the Cursor

For 40 years, the computer has waited for the cursor to blink. It waits for **Input**.
The Prompt Engineering era (2022-2024) didn't change this. It just made the input box bigger. You still had to ask. "Write me a poem." "Fix this bug."

The next era is **Zero-Click Agency**.

## Anticipatory Computing
The Studio OS does not wait. It anticipates.
*   **Contextual Awareness**: The **Architect** watches you edit a file. It notices you introduced a circular dependency. It doesn't wait for you to run a linter. It suggests a fix via a subtle notification. *Zero clicks to initiate.*
*   **The Night Shift**: While you sleep, the **Researcher** checks your reading list, finds the top 3 articles, summarizes them, and places them in your morning briefing. *Zero clicks to execute.*

## The Death of Intent
We are moving from "Explicit Intent" (Command-Click) to "Implicit Intent" (Behavior).
If I open the "Project Alpha" folder 5 times in an hour, the System infers I am focused on Alpha. It adjusts the **Council's** context automatically.

## The Risks of Autonomy
Zero-Click Agency is dangerous if unchecked. An agent that deletes files without asking is a virus, not an assistant.
This is why we built **The Chain of Command**.
*   **Low Stake**: Execute immediately (Summarize news).
*   **Medium Stake**: Propose and wait (Draft a blog post).
*   **High Stake**: Require biometrics (Deploy to production).

We are building a system that knows when to act and when to ask.
