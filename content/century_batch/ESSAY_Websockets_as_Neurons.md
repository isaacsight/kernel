# The Pulse of the Network

HTTP is request-response. It is "Call and Response."
Websockets are a continuous connection. They are a **Synapse**.

## Building a Nervous System
To make the web feel alive, we need constant state synchronization.
*   **Presence**: Knowing the agent is 'thinking' even when no text is streaming.
*   **Interrupts**: Being able to send a 'STOP' signal that arrives instantly (not on the next poll).

## The Cost of Connection
Keeping thousands of sockets open is expensive. It requires a stateful backend (FastAPI/Node).
It breaks the "Serverless" dream of stateless lambdas.
But intelligence *is* stateful.
You cannot have a relationship with a stateless function.

## The Feeling of Life
When you see a cursor move on the screen because an agent 500 miles away decided to move it, you feel a spark.
That spark is the ghost in the machine.
Websockets are the wire that carries the ghost.
