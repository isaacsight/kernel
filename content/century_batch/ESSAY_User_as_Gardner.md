# The Pruning Shears

If the AI creates the foliage (generates content, code, ideas), the User must wield the shears.
We are moving from an Economy of Creation to an **Economy of Selection**.

## The curation Interface
The primary interaction on the Studio OS is not "Typing." It is "Approving."
*   👍 **Keep**: This thought is good. Use it to train the model.
*   👎 **Prune**: This thought is bad. Discard it.
*   ✂️ **Trim**: Keep the core, cut the fluff.

## Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF)
Every time you prune, you train the local LoRA adapter.
You are teaching the garden how *you* like it to grow.
"I like concise headers." "I hate emojis."
Over time, the garden grows perfectly shaped to your taste without you lifting a finger.

## The Joy of Subtraction
There is a deep satisfaction in subtraction.
Taking a massive AI draft and cutting it down to the diamond.
It is the sculpture method. The art is in what you take away.
