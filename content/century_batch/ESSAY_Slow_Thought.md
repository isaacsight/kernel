# The Race to Zero is Wrong

The AI industry is obsessed with "Time to First Token." 
They want the answer instantly.
This is fine for "What is the weather?"
It is terrible for "How do I restructure my company?"

## Speed Kills Nuance
When you force an LLM to answer instantly, you force it to rely on **Probabilistic Caching** (System 1).
It gives you the most likely answer, which is usually the most cliché answer.
It doesn't have time to doubt itself.

## The Loading Spinner of Wisdom
We need to re-introduce friction.
The Studio OS has a mode called **"Deep Thought."**
When you ask a question, it doesn't stream.
It shows a spinner: "Thinking... (Step 5/20)."
It takes 30 seconds.
But the answer is 10x smarter.

## Buying Time
Latency is the currency of intelligence.
We are paying seconds to buy insights.
In a world of instant gratification, the willingness to wait is a competitive advantage.
Don't rush the machine.
