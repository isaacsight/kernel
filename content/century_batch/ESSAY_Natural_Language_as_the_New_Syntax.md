# Precision in English

People say, "English is too ambiguous for coding."
They are wrong. Legal contracts are written in English. They are (mostly) precise.
Prompt Engineering is just **Legalese for Machines**.

## Structural English
We are developing a pidgin English for AI.
*   "Given X, when Y, then Z." (Gherkin syntax).
*   "Constraint:", "Context:", "Instruction:".

## The New IDE
The IDE of 2030 won't highlight syntax errors.
It will highlight **Ambiguity errors**.
"Your instruction 'Make it pop' is ambiguous. Did you mean: Increase contrast? Add animation?"
The skill of the future developer is **Articulation**.
Can you say exactly what you mean?
