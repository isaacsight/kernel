# The Studio Thesis

**Does This Feel Right? is the reference implementation for an Agentic OS. It runs on a specific orchestration pattern, exposes its internal logic as an Open Core, and offers advisory guidance to teams building their own.**

## Market Context

**The market moment is NOW: Agentic AI as the new operating paradigm.**

We have shifted from "AI copilots" (autocomplete) to **agentic systems** (autonomous agents with planning, goals, and orchestration). While giants like Salesforce serve the enterprise mass market, a critical gap exists for founders who need **taste, governance, and traceability**—not just scale.

Most organizations are hitting a wall: they have agents, but they lack the *operating system* to coordinate them. They struggle with:
*   **governance** ("What did the agent do?"),
*   **alignment** ("Does this feel right?"), and
*   **orchestration** ("How do I stop agent sprawl?").

## What This Studio Is

This is a **Supervisor Pattern** reference implementation.

It demonstrates how one human orchestrates a multi-agent **Frontier Team** (Architect, Operator, Librarian) through a centralized orchestrator (Antigravity). It prioritizes **reasoning transparency and traceability** over raw speed—exactly what founders need when scaling AI systems from pilots to production.

## Where It Sits in the AI Stack

The giants (OpenAI, Anthropic) provide the models. The platforms (Salesforce) provide the plumbing.

**Does This Feel Right?** sits above them as the **governance and taste layer**: a lived example of how to configure those models into a coherent, high-trust system.

## Who It Is For

The studio is for founders and product/engineering leads who:
1.  **Have agentic systems in motion.** You aren't just prompting; you are building flows.
2.  **Feel the misalignment.** You worry about losing control, "agent sprawl," or the quality of autonomous decisions.
3.  **Want a reference OS.** You don't want a consultant to build it for you; you want a proven architecture to adapt.

## How the Studio Operates Internally

The studio runs on a **Supervisor Pattern**:

*   **Orchestrator (Antigravity)**: The central node that receives requests, decomposes them, and assigns them to specialists.
*   **Frontier Team (Specialists)**: Distinct agent roles (e.g., The Architect for system design, The Librarian for memory) that execute specific sub-tasks.
*   **Artifact-Based Review**: No work is "done" until it produces a verified artifact (code, doc, plan) that a human reviews.

This pattern ensures that every action is **observable, aligned, and reversible**.
