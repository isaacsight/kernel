# Phase 1: The OSS Mission

For the past year, "Does This Feel Right?" has operated as a private laboratory—a place where we've obsessively built and rebuilt the **Studio OS**. Today, we are breaking the seal.

We are evolving the Studio OS into a **fully free and open-source platform**.

## Why Now?
The "Implementation Gap" in AI is real. Teams have the power of models like Gemini and Claude, but they lack the nervous system to coordinate them. Most attempts at "Agentic OS" designs are either too generic or too locked-down.

By going Open Source, we are inviting the community to watch, fork, and contribute to a system that prioritizes **integrity, taste, and craft** over raw automation volume.

## The First Release: DTFR Harness
Our first open-source pillar is the **Agentic Evaluation & Observability Harness**.

It is a protocol for recording and scoring the "felt-sense" of agentic actions. It moves us away from brittle "evals" and toward a verifiable standard of trust.

- **Zero Gates**: Observability tools should be accessible to all.
- **Verifiable Judgment**: Logs that don't just show *what* happened, but *why* it felt right (or wrong).

## What's Next?
This is just Phase 1. Over the coming weeks, we will be open-sourcing our core agent architectures, decision-log protocols, and the "living lab" infrastructure that powers this studio.
