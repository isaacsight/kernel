title: Why Most Products Feel Wrong
date: 2025-12-13
category: Thinking
mode: Note
pillar: Strategy
excerpt: It's not a feature problem. It's a truth problem.
---

Most products feel wrong because they are built to extract, not to assist.

You can feel the intent in the interface.
A chaotic dashboard is a team structure problem.
A dark pattern is a revenue desperation problem.
A slow app is a respect problem.

When a founder tells me their product "doesn't strictly fit the market," they usually mean they built something dishonest. They built what they wanted to sell, not what the user needed to do.

Great products feel like they were discovered, not invented. They feel inevitable.

This is the only metric that matters:
**Does the product respect the user's intent more than the company's metrics?**

If the answer is no, you are just renting attention until someone honest comes along.
