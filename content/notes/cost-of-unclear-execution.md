---
title: The Cost of Unclear Execution
date: 2025-12-13
category: Thinking
mode: Note
pillar: Execution
excerpt: Ambiguity is the most expensive thing in your company.
---

Ambiguity is compound interest on debt you didn't know you took out.

When a CEO says "we need to be more innovative," but doesn't define the constraints, they haven't given a directive. They've created a tax.
Every engineer will now spend 20% of their energy guessing what "innovative" means.
Every designer will over-polish the wrong features.

Execution problems are rarely skill problems. They are almost always clarity problems.

If you cannot write down exactly what success looks like in three bullet points, do not ask anyone to build it.
The cost of your confusion is their burnout.

Clarity is not micromanagement. Clarity is kindness.
