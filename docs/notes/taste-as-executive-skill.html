Taste As An Executive Skill

Thinking
{{ post_context }}

"Taste" is mostly treated as a mystical, artistic quality. Something for the creatives. In reality, taste is a high-leverage executive skill.

Taste is simply pattern recognition compressed into intuition. It is the ability to look at a decision—a hire, a roadmap, a UI interaction—and instantly know if it aligns with the standard of excellence, without needing a committee meeting or an A/B test.

When a leader lacks taste, everything becomes a debate. "Why shouldn't we put a pop-up here? The data says it drives conversions." A leader with taste says: "Because it's undignified, and we don't build undignified software." Conversation over.

Taste saves time. Taste protects the brand. Taste attracts talent.

You can't A/B test your way to a soul.
