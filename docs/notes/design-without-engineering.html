Design Without Engineering Is Fiction

Thinking
{{ post_context }}

I have seen millions of dollars wasted on "Design Sprints" that resulted in beautiful, unimplementable hallucinations.

Designers who do not understand the medium of software are just digital painters. Engineers who do not respect the nuance of interaction are just typists.

The separation of "Design" and "Engineering" is a legacy industrial artifact. In software, they are the same discipline: System Definition.

If you design a UI component without understanding the data model that powers it, you haven't designed anything. You've just drawn a picture of a problem you don't understand.

The best systems are built by people who can hold both the pixel and the database schema in their head at the same time. Everything else is just a handoff. And every handoff is a signal loss.
