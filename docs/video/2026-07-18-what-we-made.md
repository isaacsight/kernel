# WHAT WE HAVE MADE — 1-minute film, production plan

School of Life-register motion graphic about the GALLEY engine.
Locked: 720p, Seedance 2.0 image-to-video, premium stills, ElevenLabs VO,
Palmier cut. Budget cap $30 all-in.

## Narration (~150 words, warm essayist voice, ~60s at measured pace)

1. Most of us have ideas far more often than we have the means to make them.
2. So we built a small engine that lives quietly on one computer.
3. You describe the picture you want, and a director refines your words.
4. The director is an intelligence called Claude, working at a canvas called GALLEY.
5. Behind them wait three hundred cameras — the world's best image and video models.
6. Nothing is filmed until the price appears, plainly, on a small card.
7. You may always say no. Refusal costs nothing at all.
8. Say yes, and the render arrives — and belongs to you, on your own disk.
9. A voice can be added for pennies. An editor cuts it to the beat.
10. The whole minute costs less than lunch.
11. The point was never automation, but delegation. The machine walks; you decide.
12. What we have made is a studio the size of a thought.

## Scenes — 12 x 5s, keyframe -> image-to-video

Shared style spec for every keyframe prompt: "flat textured paper-collage
illustration, muted ivory and ink palette with one warm tomato accent,
generous negative space, soft grain, editorial magazine style" + scene.

| # | Keyframe subject | Motion direction (minimal, anti-warble) |
